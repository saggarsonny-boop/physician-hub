About Sonny Saggar, MD

Sonny Saggar trained in England, at Oxford and at St. Bartholomew's Hospital Medical College, and spent twenty five years practicing emergency medicine before a federal indictment and incarceration. He wrote GROUND: How to Lose Everything and Still Have Somewhere to Stand by hand during his sentence in a federal prison camp, and completed it after his release. He is a physician who walked through the criminal justice system and writes about regulatory systems, administrative law, and collateral consequences from the inside.

Speaking Topics

When the Floor Gives Way: Stoicism Under Extreme Pressure

How to apply classical Stoic principles when your career, status, and routine collapse in a single afternoon. Focuses on control variables and maintaining internal direction under structural confinement.

The Difference Between Losing and Being Lost: Identity After Collapse

A guide to separating your identity from your professional credentials. Rebuilding self worth and standing firm when the external markers of success are confiscated.

Systems Autopsy: How Administrative Law Can Destroy a Career

An ER doctor's clinical look at regulatory enforcement, administrative intent standards, and post-Chevron licensing due process reviews.
